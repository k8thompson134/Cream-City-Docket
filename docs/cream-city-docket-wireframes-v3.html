About Cream City Docket
Milwaukee government, made understandable.
Cream City Docket is a civic transparency tool that monitors Milwaukee Common Council legislation and delivers plain-English summaries and timely alerts so residents can act before votes happen — not after.
Why this exists
In April 2026, the Milwaukee Common Council passed a 10 PM food truck curfew downtown. The vote was unanimous. Most residents found out after it had already passed.
That experience is common. Milwaukee's legislative data is technically public — it's all on the city's Legistar system — but it's fragmented, hard to navigate, and rarely covered by media until after decisions are final. By the time most residents learn a bill exists, the window to testify, contact their alder, or influence the outcome has already closed.
Cream City Docket exists to close that gap.
How it works
1. The system checks Milwaukee's public Legistar API every hour for new and updated legislation.
2. Each bill is summarized in plain English using Claude Haiku (Anthropic's AI).
3. Bills are tagged by issue area from a fixed taxonomy.
4. Subscribers receive email alerts when matching bills are introduced, scheduled, or voted on.
5. All original source documents link directly back to official city records.
On AI-generated summaries
Summaries on this site are generated by an AI (Claude Haiku by Anthropic) and are intended to help you understand what a bill does — not to replace the official text. Every summary links to the original filing on Legistar. If you are making a decision based on legislation, always read the source document.
You can turn off AI summaries in Settings and see the official bill analysis text instead.
What this is not
✗ Not partisan or ideologically affiliated
✗ Not a replacement for journalism
✗ Not a data harvesting tool — email addresses are only used to send alerts
✗ Not opinion disguised as fact
Data sources
Legislative data — City of Milwaukee Legistar (public API)
AI summaries — Anthropic Claude Haiku
Alder contact info — City of Milwaukee Common Council website
Election history — Milwaukee City Elections Commission

A civic glossary
Ordinance
Permanent change to Milwaukee's municipal code. Takes effect the day after publication.
Charter Ordinance
Changes the City Charter itself. Requires a 2/3 council vote and takes effect 60 days after publication.
Resolution
A one-time policy directive or statement. Doesn't change permanent law.
Committee hearing
Most bills are reviewed by a standing committee before going to the full council. Public testimony is accepted here.
Veto override
If the mayor vetoes a bill, the council can override it with 10 votes (2/3 of 15 alders).
Substitute file
When a bill is amended significantly, a new version called a "substitute" replaces the original text.

Settings

AI & Summaries
Cream City Docket uses AI to generate plain-language summaries of legislation. These settings let you control how that content appears.
Show AI-generated summaries
When on, each bill shows a plain-English summary generated by Claude Haiku.
When AI summaries are off: Bills display the official analysis text filed with the city clerk's office, truncated with a link to the full text on Legistar. This text is written by city staff or the bill's sponsor, not AI.
Show AI confidence indicator
Displays a note when a summary may be less reliable (e.g. very short or complex bill text).
Accessibility
Display preferences to improve readability and usability.
High contrast mode
Increases contrast between text and background for improved readability.
Large text mode
Increases base font size throughout the site.
Reduce motion
Disables animations and transitions for users sensitive to motion.
Dyslexia-friendly font
Switches to a typeface designed to improve readability for users with dyslexia.
